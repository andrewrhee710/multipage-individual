Where can you find more resources about sourdough?

There are tons of online resources that have more information regarding the science, art, and craft of sourdough. While there was always a sourdough following online, the COVID-19 pandemic really kickstarted the "trend" of sourdough on social media. People loved making the bread for two reasons: it was a way to bake without store-bought yeast (as it was in scarcity in some areas) and sourdough is really good! I've made a small list of my favorite bakers, articles, and more below.

Claire Saffitz

Claire is sort of a phenom in the online baking world. Working as a test cook for Bon Appetit, she quickly rose in popularity when her company started posting online cooking tutorials. Although she was well known for her exquisite baking skills, she showed off her versitlity cooking dishes like Steamed Pork Buns and Lousiana BBQ Ribs. She also partook in a massively popular series hosted by Bon Appetit, where she would try and recreate popular snacks and candy (such as Pocky, StarBursts, PizzaBagels, etc.). After the scandal that took place mid-2020 for Bon Appetit, Claie split with the company and pursued a solo career. Now with a successful cookbook launch, an appearance on late-night television, and her own YouTube channel, Claire teaches others who just have their appartment kitchens how to make all kinds of desserts with ease.
